## Tips for better anime prompts

### Style specificity

Instead of just saying "anime style," add detail about the specific look you want:

- **90s anime OVA** -- Film grain, cel shading, slightly muted colors, more detailed backgrounds
- **Modern digital anime** -- Clean lines, vivid colors, smooth gradients, high detail
- **Watercolor anime** -- Soft edges, pastel palette, painterly backgrounds (Ghibli influence)
- **Dark anime** -- High contrast, desaturated with selective color, heavy shadows

### Motion language

Anime has its own visual vocabulary for motion:

- **Speed lines** -- Radiating lines that convey velocity and impact
- **Impact frames** -- Single high-contrast frames that punctuate action
- **Flowing hair and cloth** -- Almost always in motion, even in calm scenes
- **Sakura petals or falling leaves** -- Used to convey time passing or emotional moments
- **Wind effects** -- Hair, clothing, and environmental elements responding to wind

### Camera techniques specific to anime

- **Dramatic zoom** -- Fast push-in to a character's face for shock or determination
- **Pan across still frame** -- Camera moves across a detailed still image, common in emotional scenes
- **Dutch angle** -- Tilted frame for tension and unease
- **Whip pan** -- Very fast horizontal camera movement between subjects
