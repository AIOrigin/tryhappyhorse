## Step 1: Understand what HappyHorse can reportedly do

Based on public reporting and benchmark results, HappyHorse supports several generation modes:

- **Text-to-video**: Generate video clips from text descriptions
- **Image-to-video**: Animate a still image into motion video
- **Audio-video sync**: Generate video with synchronized audio output
- **1080p output**: Full HD resolution for generated clips

Each mode has different input requirements and use cases. The [text-to-video tutorial](/happyhorse-text-to-video/) and [image-to-video tutorial](/happyhorse-image-to-video/) go deeper into each workflow.

## Step 2: Prepare your prompts

While you wait for access, prompt preparation is the most productive thing you can do. Strong AI video prompts typically include:

1. **Subject**: Who or what appears in the scene
2. **Setting**: The environment and background
3. **Motion**: What movement happens during the clip
4. **Camera**: Shot type and camera movement
5. **Mood and lighting**: Atmosphere, color palette, time of day
6. **Duration**: How long the clip should be

See the full [HappyHorse prompt templates](/happyhorse-prompts/) page for reusable examples.

## Step 3: Start with text-to-video

Text-to-video is the most accessible starting point for beginners because it requires only a written description. A basic workflow looks like this:

1. Write a clear, detailed prompt (see the formula above)
2. Select quality and resolution settings (1080p if available)
3. Choose a duration (shorter clips tend to be more coherent)
4. Generate and review the output
5. Iterate on the prompt based on what you see

For a detailed walkthrough, see [HappyHorse text-to-video tutorial](/happyhorse-text-to-video/).

## Step 4: Move to image-to-video when ready

Once you are comfortable with text-to-video, image-to-video gives you more visual control:

1. Prepare a high-quality source image
2. Write a motion prompt describing how you want the image to animate
3. Adjust motion intensity and direction if the interface allows it
4. Generate and compare output against your source

For detailed guidance, see [HappyHorse image-to-video tutorial](/happyhorse-image-to-video/).

## Step 5: Optimize your results

Based on general AI video generation best practices that should apply to HappyHorse:

- **Be specific**: Vague prompts produce vague results. Name the shot type, lighting, and motion.
- **Keep clips short**: AI video models generally produce more coherent output in shorter durations.
- **Iterate quickly**: Generate several variations with small prompt changes rather than trying to get one perfect result.
- **Use reference images**: When available, image-to-video mode gives you more control over the visual starting point.
- **Watch for artifacts**: Pay attention to hands, faces, and physics, which are common weak points for all AI video models.
